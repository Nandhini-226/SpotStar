Yuvan Shankar Raja

Indian Music Composer, Playback Singer, and Producer

Yuvan Shankar Raja

Birthdate: January 6, 1967

Hometown: Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India

Yuvan Shankar Raja, often referred to as "U1," is a renowned music composer and playback singer in Indian cinema. Known as the "King of BGM", He is the youngest son of legendary composer Ilaiyaraaja, he has carved his own unique identity in the Tamil film industry.

Career Highlights

Debut as Composer: U1 Shankar Raja made his debut as a music composer with the Tamil film Puriyaatha Puthir (2017), earning recognition for his fresh sound and innovative compositions.

Notable Films: Gained recognition for his work in films like Kuppathu Raja, Iruttu and V1. His soundtracks are known for their fusion of folk, contemporary, and experimental music.

Achievements

First Tamil composer to win the Cyprus International Film Festival Award for Raam (2005).

Frequently credited with popularizing electronic music in Tamil cinema.

Composed for over 100 films, delivering chart-topping hits in multiple genres.
