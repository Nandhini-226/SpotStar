K. J. Yesudas

Playback Singer

About

Birthdate:January 10, 1940

Hometown: Fort Kochi, Kerala, India

Dr. K. J. Yesudas is an iconic Indian playback singer often referred to as the "Gana Gandharvan" (Celestial Singer). His unmatched voice and devotion to music have made him one of the greatest singers in Indian history.

Career Highlights

Legendary Career: Sung over 80,000 songs in various languages, including Malayalam, Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, and Kannada. Iconic Songs: Famous for tracks like Harivarasanam, Manushyan Mathangale Srishtichu, and Kadalinakkare Ponore. Global Fame: Performed in international venues, showcasing Indian classical and film music.

Awards

National Film Awards: Best Playback Singer multiple times.

Padma Awards: Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan from the Government of India.

Kerala State Film Awards: Honored numerous times for his unmatched contribution to Malayalam cinema.
